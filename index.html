Problem

Half the lecture is what's visible on screen

The instructor writes a formula on the board and says "right here" — but nothing makes sense by ear alone. A screen reader will read the platform buttons, not what's in the video. So half the lecture simply disappears.

How it works

Watches, describes, answers

  1. 01

    Watches with you

    OKO sees the frame just like your sighted classmate does.

  2. 02

    Describes in the pauses

    Formulas, graphs, and actions — spoken aloud while the instructor is silent.

  3. 03

    Answers your questions

    Ask: "What's on the graph?", "Repeat the formula" — and OKO responds right during the lecture.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

For students

How much does it cost?

The beta is free for now. We want people to use it and tell us what to improve.

When can I try it?

We're launching the first betas in fall 2026. Leave your email — we'll write as soon as access opens.

What videos does OKO work with?

Lectures, tutorials, webinar recordings — any educational video where seeing the screen matters.

Does it work in Ukrainian?

Yes. We're starting with Ukrainian — because tools like this didn't exist here yet.

How is this different from regular video description?

Regular description is a recorded track, the same for everyone. OKO watches the video with you: describes in pauses, adapts pace, and answers your questions right while you watch.

For platforms and educational institutions

How does OKO integrate?

It's a plugin in your video player. No need to rework videos or upload anything upfront — OKO works on top of the library you already have.

What about legal accessibility requirements?

OKO meets the audio description requirement in WCAG 2.1 (criterion 1.2.5). That directly relates to ADA in the U.S. (deadlines already passed) and the European Accessibility Act in the EU (effective since 2025).

What about accuracy? AI makes mistakes.

For regular lectures, AI handles it well. For critical content — exams, complex formulas — there's a human-reviewed mode.

What about data privacy?

Video and student data are processed within your platform. We're designing for GDPR and FERPA requirements from day one.
